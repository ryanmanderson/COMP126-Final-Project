Programmable Button

2018-2019

By using a Raspberry Pi and a simple website, members set out to create a button that whose function could be changed on the go. When a button connected to a Raspberry Pi is pressed it performs whatever action the user has chosen from the interface of the website. The idea is to make performing actions, such as playing music, getting directions, or making phone calls, easier for indivduals with limited motor skills.

Pill Reminder

2018-2019

Google Actions are capable of providing users with small amounts of information in an efficient manner. Members worked on a Google Action of their own that was intended to remind users of what medications they needed to take on a given day. By telling the Google Action what medications they were supposed to take on what days, users could then ask their Google Assistant what pills they needed to take at any time without any worry of forgetting a prescription.

AR Navigation

2017-2018

Augmented Reality is an exciting and captivating technology. Club members were interested in using AR to make it safer and easier for individuals with intellectual disabilities to navigate on their own. By using eye-grabbing AR objects in an Android application, members attempted to make their own navigation application that used AR objects as visual aids to those seeking walking directions to a desired destination.

Morehead Captioning

2016-2017

This app is designed with the Morehead Planetarium as an aid to those that are hearing impaired by displaying transcriptions to Google Glass. The goal is to have two modes for the two types of shows done at the planetarium: (1) transcribe script from a pre-written show to be displayed at corresponding timestamps (2) capture speech in real-time to be transcribed and displayed immediately for live shows.
